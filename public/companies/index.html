Clout
Fan engagement platform — gamifying fandom.

Clout is the B2C fan engagement platform that rewards fans for showing up. A dual-currency economy (XP + Coins), pick’em games, and digital challenges transform passive sports fans into active participants who have real skin in the game.

The Clout OS powers everything underneath — a multi-tenant infrastructure layer that lets sports organizations white-label the entire fan engagement experience under their own brand. Clout B2C is the flagship tenant, proving the model before it licenses to others.

Inside PorterLabs, Clout is the most fully-deployed company in the recursive system — with an active Polaris node, a Marketing Node running SMOS at 7–10 posts per day, and a Sales Node driving outbound to sports organizations on the B2B licensing play. A 35–40% margin model on services makes the B2B side structurally attractive at scale.

Triplebolt
Product engineering — building what the system requires.

Triplebolt is PorterLabs’ product engineering arm — the Thundr layer made real. When Node tasks inside PorterLabs require engineering to move forward, Triplebolt delivers. It operates on the operating state Polaris sets, not on ad-hoc requests.

Its defining output so far is Clout-OS — the multi-tenant fan engagement platform that powers Clout’s entire B2B play. A single build that unlocked an entire licensing model. That’s the Triplebolt pattern: scoped, delivered, handed back.

Beyond its role inside PorterLabs, Triplebolt operates as a delivery partner for Clout Engineering’s B2B services engagements — AI, automation, and operational efficiency for sports organizations. Three Curve Capital holds approximately 30% ownership.

Kairix
Intelligent systems for healthcare — starting with referral management.

Kairix is building intelligent operating infrastructure for hospitals and healthcare systems. The first solution is a referral management system for Barrow Brain & Spine — one of the leading neuroscience institutes in the country. When a patient is referred into Barrow, Kairix routes, tracks, and surfaces that patient through the intake process with precision.

Kairix is a PorterLabs venture, with Stu holding ownership. The build is being delivered by Triplebolt, which means the same engineering pattern that produced Clout-OS is now applied to healthcare infrastructure. The recursive system carries the advantage across verticals.

Once the first solution ships and goes live at Barrow, Kairix graduates to its own Polaris node inside the PorterLabs recursive system — with a full complement of operating Nodes configured for the healthcare vertical. The graduation trigger is the first solution going live.

  • First solution Referral management system

Entering Atrium
BarberStack
Operations platform for independent barbers and shops.

BarberStack is the second resident of the PorterLabs Atrium — in Phase 1, building the foundation. The independent barber and shop owner is an underserved operator: talented, busy, and running their business on fragmented tools, word-of-mouth, and intuition. BarberStack is built to change that.

The same system-powered approach that produced Clout’s infrastructure now serves a new vertical. Before any code is written, BarberStack must complete the Atrium’s six required Phase 1 documents — the same documentation and validation gate that every PorterLabs company passes through before engineering begins.

When Phase 1 is approved and the first version ships with real traction, BarberStack graduates to its own Polaris node inside the recursive system — joining Clout and Kairix as a fully-deployed company with functional Nodes running autonomously.

Key Facts
  • Stage Atrium Phase 1 — documentation and validation
  • Vertical Independent barbers and shops
  • Phase 1 docs Six required documents in progress
  • Code gate Engineering begins after Phase 1 approval
  • Graduation trigger Own Polaris node on first traction signal
